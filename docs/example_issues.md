My 1st Atlantic crossing East to West & Some Pointers for Crew:
This is one of those difficult situations to decide in how far one shares the REAL experience on this past Atlantic crossings on the 1983 build 40ft steel sloop LA BALLENA, or keep it social media like and just offer some advise how to hopefully avoid repeat experiences by others.
Despite the extremely unpleasant experiences with numerous accounts of at times severe verbal outbreaks by the skipper mostly targeted towards the two other younger crew; I decided to only share my recommendations on what to look out for and what warning signals one should take serious especially prior to a three week crossing during which one is totally exposed to the, in this case unfortunately eccentric and volatile mood swings of an immature Captain and confined to the few square meters of a old, and in my eyes 'not quite ready' boat.
Calling a specific crew member a “fuc.... wan...” and “I just want to smash his face in...” towards other crew members are certainly extreme examples of very poor communication skills by a skipper.
For me this was my first Atlantic crossing on another boat but I have been crewing for many many years internationally on other people’s boats for offshore races and cruising; so I have a very healthy understanding what is ok and what it definitely off the scale of ok!
During my more than 40 years of racing, cruising and enjoying sailing I have never experienced anything like it… and wish for nobody to experience it either.
Did we have some fun, YES - fortunately the other crew members were very capable and contributed to an overall super adventure!
So, herewith a few pointers for any of you crew looking to join a boat for any adventures and / or longer passages:
LOGISTICS
Sign a mutual indemnity agreement. You are risking your life and trusting the skipper with a safe boat; which skippers seem to sometime forget. I have unfortunately witnessed a skipper continuously insinuating blame onto crew for old / ill maintained or faulty equipment. Including verbal statements “I’ll get my money for it from them!”...
There should be part of the agreement that states a mutual understanding of healthy conduct between all people on board that strongly discourages foul language against anybody on board and banning any form of, or threat of physical and verbal aggression.
Ask if skipper will do a quick ‘Any issues round’ every day or how any friction or conflicts would be addressed in a mature and relaxed way.
Who are the other crew, age, couples or singles and their experience levels?
What is the intended watch sequence? How will any time zone shift be dealt with and how will the time shift be shared?
Check travel onward from destination, is this a good location to get connected or reach another boat where you like to go?
Discuss and agree if your travel to and from is shared or paid for?
Funny one; ask for bunk size and headroom at the helm positions if you are tall 😅
Do you need to bring towels, bedding, detergents?
BOAT / CAPTAIN BACKGROUND CHECK
Ask to see a copy of the insurance policy of the boat.
Check captain out on social media; a lot can be deducted from feedback and overall impressions
Ask for his navigational qualifications, VHF license, safety training
Does he have sufficient sailing experience?
Is he a balanced and mature character to spend three-four weeks together on a tiny space? Initial impressions on communication styles can tell a lot…
Has he had the boat he is intending to cross the Atlantic with for more than a year in operation actually proving off-shore capability and readiness?
Has he done any serious miles since purchase?
EQUIPMENT
When was the rigging last professionally inspected? I would even request to see a copy in future. In this instance I did a full rigging inspection myself.
Are the navigational instruments working and present? Or is the wind instrument flags on the stays and the windex covered by the solar panels?
Ask what the service status of primary systems are.
Does the steering work? When last professionally serviced?
Does the engine start/work? When last professionally serviced?
Do the solar panels produce enough power to cook and make coffee or is it just on all sunny days without use of autopilot? In my experience 1500-1800watt installed is minimum depending if desalination is on board or not.
Is the compass calibrated and illuminated for night navigation?
SAFETY
Wear your life vest certainly from dusk to dawn and anytime you are outside of the cockpit! I have seen too many near overboard happenings...
Wear shoes when sail handling / deck work especially if the boat is more old fashioned with many steel fittings and painted anti-skid deck surfaces.
Have some form of very noisy audible alarm / blow horn available at the helm - in case all crew are required on deck in case of a breakage or emergency.
Will the vessel engage in continuous channel 16 monitoring? For possible help requests / warnings from / by others boaters?
Are the mob and rescue equipment working and within date?
Is there a substantial medical kit on board?
Life raft and grab bag present and located close to entry / escape position?
Emergency rescue location beacon?
ESSENTIALS
Make sure to get a good tour or video showing of the boat including your designated bunk / cabin prior to agreeing anything.
Is the only toilet working? Or do you need to flush it with a bucket unfortunately dropped overboard by the skipper (2)?
What capacity of drinking water is intended to be available on board? Make the calculation yourself and check!
Water consumption; take bare minimum 3ltr / day for drinking water and another 3ltr / day for just cooking and bare teeth brushing and rinsing (salt water showers) per person. Fresh water showers require desalination plant supply.
CONTRIBUTION
Define in a written agreement what is included in and what the potential contribution is set at and if it’s per day or week / per person or couple if going as couple?
When is the contribution to be paid and in cash or bank transfer?
What about harbor fees? What if departure is delayed due to ill repair condition of boat and/or waiting for replacement parts to arrive? Will you still be required to pay share the harbour fees???
Ask for your passports back after each harbour check in/out.
Port handling fees? Shared or part of the contribution?
Will you be asked to contribute Starlink data or even subscription fees? Note that its certainly good practice to disable automatic updates, cloud uploading and downloading of media in any case.
Will it be available throughout the trip or randomly just a few minutes per day at times you are mostly helming?
Define what are the expected shared duties? Are you expected to refit the captains boat or just help with normal stuff like cooking, keeping boat tidy, helming, night navigation and sail operations?
Is all food included? Discuss dietary preferences. Will you be asked to pay for muesli, nuts or almond milk, but not get credit for not consuming any more expensive fish and meat products?
Discuss if Alcohol will be available during the transit, and if so how is it shared and who is responsible to keep an eye on safe consumption. On our crossing we had agreed to not consume any which for me was totally fine and actually welcomed.
I recommend to agree on a structured routine with fixed turns for meal preparations, cleaning duties and free / floating times (unless required for deck works / sail changes)
Final clear out and clean up; its typical to share a general clean up and/or to find a launderette at your anchor destination to wash the bed linens in addition?
Hot Tip for skippers: plan for Caribbean sailing to be with moonlight as squalls are much easier to see at night.
Is the boat / skipper prepared to encounter some serious amounts of seaweed most of the times in smaller patches or strings of, but also in larger patches of more than a mile in size? Long keel hulls with hung rudders fair much better but still the Windpilot rudder was affected in our case.
For fresh fish we caught about 12 Mahi Mahi ranging from 3-11kg, one 4kg Tuna and a small Wahoo which we returned... it was a welcoming and very tasty menu extension as towards the last week the provisions were getting limited to a rather narrow selection of beans, pasta and rice.
I hope this is useful to some of you and foremost I wish everyone a safe and enjoyable sailing adventure! ⛵️⚓️🌈🦈🧜‍♀️🍾
